The problem

The bottleneck isn't your software. It's the layer between teams.

Most companies already have CRMs, helpdesks, dashboards, and internal tools. But work still depends on people forwarding context, chasing approvals, and clarifying ownership.

As companies grow, this coordination layer becomes invisible infrastructure. Execution slows because no system is responsible for carrying work across the finish line.

  • Coordination becomes a full-time job at scale.
  • Work gets stuck between functions, not inside them.
  • Teams spend too much time moving work and not enough time finishing it.
  • Systems store information, but they do not execute the workflow.
What we're building

A system of execution, not just record.

Most business software captures data or displays status. Sheldon acts on that information by maintaining context, deciding what happens next, and pushing each workflow toward completion.

Not another dashboard Not another ticketing tool Not another place to update status manually
  • Understands requests across teams and systems.
  • Maintains context across the full lifecycle of a workflow.
  • Routes tasks dynamically to the right owners and tools.
  • Automates approvals, follow-ups, and escalations.
  • Tracks workflow state in real time.
  • Improves from historical workflow data over time.

An AI-native operating system that runs itself.

1Ingest

Why now

AI can finally operate the layer people have been patching manually.

For years, companies solved operational complexity by hiring coordinators, adding new tools, or creating process overhead around the gaps. That approach does not scale well, and it breaks faster as the business grows.

AI changes the equation because software can now understand requests, retain context, reason across systems, and drive work forward. That makes it possible to build an AI-native operating system that does more than record what happened - it helps make the work happen.

The shift
From systems that store what happened - to a system responsible for making the work happen.
